The problem

Preference cards optimize for the average case. Nobody prepares for this patient, this case.

Outdated, copied-and-reused setup lists drive simultaneous waste and risk — the most common and most preventable causes of intraoperative disruption.

37%
of surgical errors linked to missing equipment
43%
linked to incorrect equipment configuration or setup
30%
longer OR occupancy when the team lacks the operative plan
11%
of side errors directly attributable to patient positioning

Equipment-related problems remain among the most common causes of intraoperative disruption; insufficient knowledge of the operative plan exacerbates complications.

From scattered inputs to one prescriptive prep plan.

The platform

Five things that have to be right. NOLO gets them right, together.

Materials & instruments

Dynamic pick lists tuned to the case — drop the ~40% of card items never used, stage what this patient actually needs.

People & staffing

Right team, right roles — no one left out. Surface the operative plan so everyone walks in prepared.

Energy devices & towers

Laparoscopic towers and energy devices configured correctly the first time — cutting the 43% of setup-related errors.

Patient positioning

Prescribed positioning per procedure and patient — directly attacking side-errors and positioning complications.

Patient-specific safety

Allergy swaps, anticoagulation hemostatics, conversion-to-open trays — comorbidity-driven flags surfaced at prep time.

Inventory & cost intelligence

Real-time inventory awareness cuts waste, delays, and cancellations — and scales into full perioperative decision support.
